Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam (1931 – 2015)

Biography

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, into a humble Tamil Muslim family in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. Growing up, he faced financial hardships and sold newspapers at an early age to supplement his family's income. Despite these obstacles, Kalam was driven by a deep curiosity and an insatiable desire to learn, particularly in the fields of physics and mathematics.

He graduated in physics from Saint Joseph's College in Tiruchirappalli and pursued aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology. After graduating, Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and was later transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). At ISRO, he served as the Project Director for India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III), which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in orbit in 1980.

During the 1980s and 1990s, Kalam directed the Integrated Guided Missile Development Programme, leading the engineering and testing of crucial strategic defense systems including Prithvi and Agni missiles. His scientific leadership and technical management skills earned him the moniker "Missile Man of India." In 1998, he served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and played a central organizational role in the Pokhran-II nuclear tests.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his tenure, he earned the title of the "People's President" due to his approachable nature, simple lifestyle, and profound connection with the youth of the country. Following his presidency, he dedicated his life to education, writing, and public service, lecturing at premier institutions and inspiring millions of students before passing away while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong on July 27, 2015.
